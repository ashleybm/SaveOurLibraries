Using Your Postcards Effectively

Once you receive your postcards, here are some tips for using them effectively:

Writing Tips

  • Keep your message brief and focused
  • Include a personal story about why libraries matter to you
  • Be specific about what action you want the recipient to take
  • Write legibly or print your message
  • Include your return address so they know you're a constituent

Sending Tips

  • Research the correct mailing address for your representatives
  • Postage included: If you order postcards through our program, they come with enough postage, sometimes preapplied
  • Consider coordinating with others to send postcards on the same day for greater impact
  • Take a photo of your postcard before sending and share on social media with #SaveOurLibraries2025

Who to Send To

  • People You Know: Personal connections who might not be aware of the threat to libraries
  • Family Members: Relatives across different generations who may have strong connections to libraries
  • Friends: Your social circle who can amplify your message and take action themselves
  • Coworkers: Workplace connections to spread awareness across professional networks

For more detailed guidance on what to write, visit our Postcard Writing Tips resource page.

The Impact of Postcards

Handwritten postcards are a powerful advocacy tool for several reasons:

  • Personal touch: Handwritten messages stand out in an age of digital communication
  • Physical presence: Postcards take up space in an office and create a visual impact
  • Constituent evidence: They provide tangible evidence of constituent concerns
  • Accessibility: Writing postcards is an advocacy method accessible to people of all ages and abilities

When many people send postcards about the same issue, it demonstrates widespread community concern and can influence decision-making.
