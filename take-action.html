Take Action Now

Urgent steps to oppose the executive order targeting libraries

Stopping the Executive Order

On March 14, 2025, the White House issued an executive order "CONTINUING THE REDUCTION OF THE FEDERAL BUREAUCRACY" that explicitly targets the Institute of Museum and Library Services (IMLS) for elimination. Section 2(a)(iv) of the order mandates that IMLS be reduced "to the minimum presence and function required by law."

This is not a vague threatâ€”it's a direct attack on the primary federal funding source for libraries nationwide. Your immediate action is crucial to prevent this order from being fully implemented and to protect library services across the country.

Make Your Voice Heard

1. Call Your Representatives

Phone calls are one of the most effective ways to influence elected officials. Use our phone scripts that specifically address the March 14, 2025 executive order.

Who to Call:

  • Your U.S. Senators (crucial for opposing this federal action)
  • Your U.S. Representative
  • White House Comment Line: 202-456-1111
  • Office of Management and Budget: 202-395-3080

What to Say:

  • Identify yourself as a constituent
  • Express your strong opposition to the March 14, 2025 executive order "CONTINUING THE REDUCTION OF THE FEDERAL BUREAUCRACY"
  • Specifically mention Section 2(a)(iv) targeting the Institute of Museum and Library Services
  • Demand that they take action to protect IMLS funding and oppose implementation of this order
  • Share how libraries impact you and your community

3. Attend Meetings

Show up at public meetings where library funding and policies are discussed.

Meetings to Attend:

  • Library board meetings
  • City council meetings
  • County commission meetings
  • School board meetings (for school libraries)
  • Budget hearings

How to Participate:

  • Sign up for public comment periods
  • Prepare a brief (1-2 minute) statement
  • Bring supporters with you
  • Follow up with written comments

Spread the Word

What to Share:

View Social Media Scripts

2. Engage Your Community

Start conversations about libraries in your community.

Ways to Engage:

  • Host a library advocacy meetup
  • Distribute information at community events
  • Write letters to the editor of local publications
  • Speak at community organization meetings

Key Messages:

  • Libraries provide essential services for everyone
  • Library funding is a smart investment in the community
  • Libraries support education, employment, and equity
  • Libraries need community support to thrive

3. Support Library Programs

Show your support by actively participating in library services.

Ways to Support:

  • Get a library card and use it regularly
  • Attend library programs and events
  • Volunteer at your local library
  • Join your library's Friends group or foundation
  • Donate to library fundraisers

Why It Matters:

  • Increased usage statistics help justify funding
  • Active community participation demonstrates value
  • Personal experiences enrich your advocacy
